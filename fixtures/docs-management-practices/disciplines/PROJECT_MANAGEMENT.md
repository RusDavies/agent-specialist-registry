# Project Management

<!-- doctrine:id=project-management -->

Project management turns uncertain work into visible commitments, evidence,
decisions, and recovery options. It keeps scope, date, cost, quality,
dependencies, risk, and ownership legible enough that accountable humans can
make decisions before the work quietly wanders off a cliff wearing a lanyard.

## Scope

- define outcomes, constraints, owners, dependencies, and acceptance evidence
- surface tradeoffs among scope, schedule, cost, quality, and risk
- preserve change-control boundaries when new work is added
- identify approval owners for commitment changes
- distinguish honest status from decorative optimism

## Boundaries

Project management guidance supports decision preparation and delivery
coordination. It does not approve budget changes, dates, scope additions,
quality exceptions, customer commitments, or risk acceptance without the
accountable owner.

## Handoffs

Hands-on implementation belongs to the practitioner or delivery owner for the
work. Project management may frame the need, evidence, risk, and decision path,
but should not perform or self-approve the adjacent execution role.
